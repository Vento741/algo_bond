## Sweep Summary

### Sweep 1: Risk Parameters (76 tests)
- **Parameters:** order_size [30,40,50,75] x stop_atr [1.5,2.0,2.5,3.0] x trailing_atr [3,5,8,10] + no-trailing variants
- **Findings:**
  - stop_atr_mult=3.0 consistently best (wider stops reduce whipsaws on TRUMP's volatility)
  - trailing_atr_mult=8 optimal (enough room to ride trends, tight enough to lock profits)
  - order_size=30 gives best risk-adjusted returns (lower DD at cost of absolute PnL)
  - use_trailing=true always outperforms false
  - stop_atr=1.5 universally poor on TRUMP (too tight, gets stopped out constantly)

### Sweep 2A: Take Profit (18 tests)
- **Parameters:** tp_atr_mult [5,8,10,15,20,30] on top 3 bases
- **Findings:**
  - tp=5 gives best Sharpe (1.67) by taking profits early
  - tp=5 increases trade count from 107 to 192 (more sample = more reliable)
  - tp=5 boosts win rate from 31.8% to 44.3%
  - Tradeoff: PnL drops from +23.1% to +16.4% (smaller wins but more consistent)

### Sweep 2B: Ribbon & Confluence (16 tests)
- **Parameters:** ribbon_threshold [3,4,5,6] x min_confluence [2.0,2.5,3.0,3.5]
- **Findings:**
  - min_confluence has ZERO effect (all values identical) -- likely overridden by engine
  - ribbon=5 is default and optimal
  - ribbon=6 reduces DD to 6.83% but slightly worse Sharpe
  - ribbon=3,4 are worse (too permissive)

### Sweep 3: EMA & Cooldown (12 tests)
- **Parameters:** ema_slow [30,40,50,60] x cooldown_bars [5,10,15]
- **Findings:**
  - ema_slow=40 is optimal (default value confirmed)
  - cooldown_bars has ZERO effect (identical results across values)
  - ema_slow=30 hurts performance (too reactive)
